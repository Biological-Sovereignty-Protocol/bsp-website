# Join the Community

## Welcome to the BSP community

The BSP community is a growing network of developers, researchers, healthcare professionals, and individuals who believe that biological sovereignty is a fundamental human right.

## Why get involved?

### Build what matters

BSP is infrastructure for the most personal data that exists. Every contribution — code, documentation, translation, feedback — moves the needle on human autonomy.

### Shape the protocol

Anyone can propose changes via BIPs (BSP Improvement Proposals). The protocol roadmap is open. Your expertise in genomics, cryptography, healthcare, or UX can directly improve BSP.

### Connect with a rare community

People working at the intersection of open protocols, privacy, longevity, and biological data are rare. BSP attracts them.

## How to get involved

### Contribute to the protocol

Read open issues, propose BIPs, or review existing proposals. All specification work happens in the open.

[View GitHub](https://github.com/biological-sovereignty-protocol) · [Open Issues](https://github.com/biological-sovereignty-protocol/bsp-spec/issues)

### Build on BSP

Use the TypeScript or Python SDK to build health apps, research tools, or data integrations on top of the protocol.

[Developer Docs](/developers/sdk-reference) · [Quickstart](/getting-started/quickstart)

### Contribute to documentation

Translations, improvements, new guides — documentation contributions are always welcome.

[Contributing Guide](https://github.com/biological-sovereignty-protocol/bsp-spec/blob/main/CONTRIBUTING.md)

### Propose a BIP

Have an idea to improve BSP? Write a BIP. The community reviews and votes on all protocol changes.

[View BIPs](/bips/) · [Submit a BIP](https://github.com/biological-sovereignty-protocol/bsp-spec/issues/new)

## Built by the community

BSP is maintained by contributors across research, healthcare, cryptography, and software engineering. No single company controls the roadmap.

[View contributors on GitHub](https://github.com/biological-sovereignty-protocol)
