# Take Permanent Ownership of Your Health Data

Your health data is the most personal information you produce. Right now, almost none of it belongs to you.

## The Problem With Your Medical Data Today

Every time you run a blood panel, visit a specialist, or sync a wearable device, you generate data. That data lives in hospital systems, lab portals, insurance databases, and app servers — all of them controlled by third parties who set the rules on access, retention, and monetization.

The practical consequences:

- **You can't take it with you.** Switching providers means starting from scratch. Years of longitudinal data vanish behind proprietary walls.
- **You can't verify it.** No cryptographic proof that the record hasn't been altered. You trust the institution, or you don't.
- **You can't share it on your terms.** Sharing requires navigating each institution's portal. There's no universal key — only individual opt-ins.
- **It can be deleted.** Platforms shut down. Hospitals merge. Retention policies expire. Your data disappears without notice.

This isn't a privacy problem in the abstract. It's a structural design flaw in how health infrastructure was built — around institutional convenience, not individual rights.

## How BSP Gives You Permanent Ownership

BSP (Biological Sovereignty Protocol) introduces a new primitive: the **BEO** (Biological Entity Object).

Your BEO is a cryptographically signed record anchored on **Arweave** — a permanent, decentralized storage network. Once written, it cannot be altered or deleted. It belongs to your cryptographic identity, not to any platform.

The ownership chain works like this:

1. You generate or import health data (lab results, biomarkers, wearable exports, medical records).
2. The data is structured into a BEO using BSP's open schema.
3. The BEO is signed with your private key and written to Arweave.
4. You hold the keys. You control access. The record persists indefinitely.

No intermediary can revoke your access. No shutdown can erase your history. No API deprecation can cut off your data pipeline.

## What You Can Do With Your BEO

Permanent ownership changes what's possible:

**Longitudinal tracking.** A decade of biomarker history, intact and portable. Feed it into any compatible tool without re-uploading or re-authorizing.

**Selective disclosure.** Grant read access to a specific provider for a specific window using BSP's consent token system. Revoke it when the window closes — cryptographically, not just by clicking "unsubscribe."

**AI integration.** Structured BEO data is machine-readable by design. Connect it to longevity AI engines, precision nutrition tools, or personal health dashboards without format conversion.

**Inheritance and delegation.** Assign trusted parties to access your BEO under defined conditions — family members, care proxies, research institutions you choose to support.

**Cross-border continuity.** Your data travels with you. No re-registration, no translation between incompatible systems. One identity, every jurisdiction.
